# Complete UI/UX + Marketing Audit
## Regression Defence Tech Microsite (`apps/defence-fund`)

**Audit Date:** April 29, 2026  
**Prepared by:** Head of UI/UX Design & Marketing Perspective  
**Primary Goal:** Improve trust, clarity, and qualified conversion from founders and investors.

---

## 1) Executive Summary

This microsite has a premium visual tone and a disciplined strategic voice, which fits the defence-tech investment space well. The core issue is not brand quality—it is **conversion readiness**.

Today, the site communicates *what you believe* better than *how a qualified person should engage next*. Visitors who are potentially high-value (serious founders, disciplined investors) may leave with respect, but without taking action.

### Most important gaps
1. **Proof deficit:** Strong claims, limited validating evidence.
2. **Flow friction:** Narrow CTA journey and no role-based pathing.
3. **Low information scent:** Many sections are abstract before they become practical.
4. **Underpowered contact funnel:** Contact asks for email outreach but does not explain process or expectations clearly.
5. **Diligence UX missing:** No FAQ/process/legal discoverability pattern for serious evaluators.

### Priority outcome target (next 60 days)
- Increase qualified founder/investor inbound by **30–50%**.
- Increase homepage-to-contact completion by **20%+**.
- Reduce thesis/criteria page exits by **15–25%**.

---

## 2) What Is Already Working

### Strengths worth preserving
- **Positioning clarity:** “Institutional readiness” is differentiated and credible.
- **Visual quality:** Dark premium aesthetic aligns with category seriousness.
- **Consistency of tone:** Professional, restrained, and compliance-aware.
- **Strategic focus:** Clear intent around concentration, selectivity, and long-term orientation.

Do **not** dilute these strengths. Improvements should add specificity and conversion mechanics without changing the brand posture.

---

## 3) Audience and Intent Mapping

You currently serve two audiences with one message stream:

1. **Founders (supply side)**
   - Need to know if they qualify.
   - Need confidence you can help materially.
   - Need low-friction but credible submission process.

2. **Investors/LP-style partners (capital side)**
   - Need process discipline and risk framing.
   - Need trust and governance signals.
   - Need clarity on access model and diligence quality.

### Recommendation
Create explicit dual entry points in hero and top navigation:
- **I’m a Founder**
- **I’m an Investor**

Each should have role-specific copy, proof, FAQs, and CTA endpoints.

---

## 4) Homepage UX/Copy Audit

## Current state diagnosis
- Hero headline is strong, but broad.
- Primary CTA (“View Thesis”) works for low urgency users, not high-intent users ready to engage.
- Mid-page sections are conceptually strong but repetitive in framing.
- “Engage With Us” appears late and lacks process expectations.

## Improvements

### 4.1 Above-the-fold rewrite structure
- Eyebrow: Keep category signal.
- Headline: Keep institutional framing.
- Subhead: Add concrete promise of process outcomes.
- CTA set:
  - **Primary:** Start Founder Intake
  - **Secondary:** Request Investor Brief
  - **Tertiary link:** View Thesis

### 4.2 Add trust strip directly under hero
Include 3–5 short items such as:
- Operator-led diligence model
- Governance-first portfolio support
- Compliance-first approach
- Selective concentration strategy

### 4.3 Add “How We Work” process block
A 4-step horizontal process:
1. Screen
2. Evaluate
3. Institutionalise
4. Align Capital

Each with one-line outcome.

### 4.4 Add qualification cards
Two cards:
- “Founder Fit” with checklist.
- “Investor Fit” with checklist.

This reduces unqualified inbound and improves conversion quality.

## 7) Conversion Funnel Design

## Current funnel
Home → Thesis/Criteria → Contact

This is linear and thin.

## Recommended funnel architecture

### Path A: Founder
Home → Founder Fit → Intake Form → Confirmation + next steps

### Path B: Investor
Home → Investor Brief Request → Qualification Questions → Follow-up

### Path C: Research-mode user
Home → Thesis/Focus/FAQ → Soft CTA (download brief) → nurture

## Conversion assets to add
- Founder Intake PDF checklist
- Investor Brief one-pager
- FAQ page with objections handling

---

## 8) Trust, Proof, and Risk Handling

## Missing trust elements
- Team credibility indicators
- Case snapshots (even anonymized)
- Process artifacts (what diligence looks like)

## Recommended proof stack (in order)
1. Team/operator background snippets
2. Process depth indicators
3. Engagement outcomes (anonymized)
4. Compliance/legal clarity

### Suggested case snapshot format
- Situation
- Constraint
- Institutionalisation work done
- Outcome achieved

Keep this concise and lawful. No promotional overclaiming.

## 11) Accessibility & Readability Improvements

Even premium B2B sites need baseline accessibility quality.

### Checklist
- Ensure heading hierarchy is strict (H1 → H2 → H3).
- Validate color contrast for gray text on dark background.
- Ensure CTA buttons have clear focus and hover states.
- Keep paragraph width and line-height optimized for long-form reading.

## 16) Final Recommendation

Do not redesign the brand; **operationalize the journey**.

The site already communicates seriousness. What it needs now is conversion mechanics that match the sophistication of the audience:
- clearer entry paths,
- stronger proof,
- concrete criteria,
- and a visible, trustworthy process.

If you implement only the top 5 recommendations, you should see measurable improvement in inbound quality and conversion within one quarter.
